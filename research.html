My research focuses on trustworthy machine learning and the Rashomon Effect. I develop algorithms that are not only accurate but also interpretable, robust, and safe for high-stakes decision-making.

My recent work centers on the Rashomon Effect, the phenomenon that many different models can achieve nearly the same optimal performance on a given dataset. We design methods to enumerate, visualize, and analyze these near-optimal models and study how model diversity supports multi-objective learning and shapes the development and deployment of modern large models.


Areas: Machine Learning, Optimization, Human-Model Interaction

Topics: Interpretability, Rashomon Effect and Model Multiplicity

Applications: Healthcare, Computational Biology
